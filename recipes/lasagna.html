Classic Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian baked pasta dish made with layers of rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese, pasta sheets, and melted mozzarella. It is hearty, comforting, and perfect for family meals or special occasions.

This version uses a slow-cooked beef sauce combined with herbs and tomatoes to create a deep, rich flavor. The layers bake together into a soft, cheesy, and satisfying dish.

Ingredients

For the meat sauce

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 cloves garlic (minced)
  • 500g ground beef
  • 1 can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For the layers

  • 9–12 lasagna pasta sheets
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese (grated)
  • 1 cup ricotta or béchamel sauce
  • ½ cup parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté onion until soft.
  2. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add ground beef and cook until browned.
  4. Stir in tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, salt, and pepper.
  5. Simmer for 20–30 minutes until thick and rich.
  6. Boil pasta sheets if needed (follow package instructions).
  7. Preheat oven to 180°C (350°F).
  8. Layer sauce, pasta, ricotta, and mozzarella in a baking dish.
  9. Repeat layers until ingredients are finished.
  10. Top with cheese and bake for 35–40 minutes.
  11. Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving.
